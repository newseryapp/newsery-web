What Is a Better Alternative to Infinite-Scrolling News Feeds?

Newsery Editorial · Feb 25, 2026

Infinite scroll is the single most disruptive design pattern in news. Here is what works better.

Why Infinite Scroll Is a Problem

Infinite scroll removes natural stopping points. It was designed to increase time-in-app, not reading quality. Without a visible end, it is difficult to feel done — and difficulty feeling done creates low-level stress that accumulates over a session.

What Finite Feeds Feel Like

A finite feed has a bottom. You scroll, you reach the end, you know you have seen what there is to see. This small change — a visible end — changes the experience of reading from open-ended obligation to completed task.

Alternatives to Infinite Scroll

  • Daily briefings: a fixed set of stories, usually email-based
  • RSS readers with folder-based management
  • Manually curated newsletters
  • Apps with finite feeds and topic control

Each option has tradeoffs. Daily briefings are passive. RSS requires maintenance. Newsletters are asynchronous. Finite feed apps offer the most daily control.

How Newsery Uses Finite Feeds

Every feed in Newsery contains up to 50 articles — a fixed, finite list based on your chosen categories and priority weights. There is no infinite scroll. When you reach the bottom, you are done. This is deliberate. It means you can read the news without the feeling that there is always more you should be seeing.
